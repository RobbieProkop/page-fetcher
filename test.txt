          <div class="column-2">
            <h2>Why We Started</h2>
            <p>Nepal Life Improvement Foundation officially formed in 2019. Prior to this, our founder Maury
              Mason, had been helping those in the community of Pachabaya, a village 200 kilometers west of
              Kathmandu, near the city of Pokhara since 2015. The contributions were to help people recover
              from the earthquake damage and resulting economic hardships of that year. The first projects
              began with rebuilding and repairing damaged homes and expanded to projects improving village
              infrastructure including pathways, stairs and connecting homes to water and hydro. In
              May 2019, along with Robbie Prokop we were officially incorporated as a Canadian Non
              Profit Organization. In 2020 Lilly Mason came on board, and helped to expand our fundraising
              base which has enabled us to take on more projects.</p>

          <div class="column-2">
            <h2>Pachabhaiya Community Development Centre</h2>
            <p>
              We have been approached by local community leaders with an opportunity to take over a large three story building that was recently left vacant. <br>
              We are converting it to a Community Resource Centre that we will use to host a variety of educational and skill development workshops and programs, including a demonstration permaculture garden where locals can learn sustainable agriculture. Other workshop plans include bee keeping, jewellery making, literacy classes, English classes, home repair and tool training, environmental stewardship and more. We have begun facilitating weekly fitness classes and art classes for children. Management and Program coordination is on a volunteer basis, however we will provide income for local labour and workshop instructors. 
   
            </p>
